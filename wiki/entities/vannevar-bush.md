# Vannevar Bush

## Overview

American engineer and science administrator who wrote the visionary 1945 essay "As We May Think," describing the Memex concept. His work anticipated hypertext, personal computers, the Internet, and modern knowledge management systems, directly inspiring the LLM Wiki pattern.

## Key Information

- **Era**: 1945 (post-World War II)
- **Role**: Scientist, engineer, science administrator
- **Major contribution**: Memex concept and "As We May Think" essay
- **Philosophy**: 
  - Scientists should focus on making knowledge accessible rather than destruction
  - Machines should handle routine work; humans should focus on creative, intuitive tasks
  - Transform "information explosion" into "knowledge explosion"
  - Knowledge management should use associative indexing (like human thought) rather than hierarchical classification
- **Historical context**: Concerned with post-WWII direction of science; advocated for peaceful applications of technology

- **Doug Engelbart**: Read essay shortly after publication; led to invention of mouse, word processor, hyperlink
- **Hypertext community**: Essay laid foundation for hypertext and web concepts
- **Andrej Karpathy**: LLM Wiki pattern explicitly references Memex as inspiration
