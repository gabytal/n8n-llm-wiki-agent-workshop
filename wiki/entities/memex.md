# Memex

## Overview

Theoretical personal knowledge management machine described by Vannevar Bush in 1945. A desk-sized device using microfilm storage and associative indexing to create "trails" of connected information. Direct conceptual ancestor of the LLM Wiki pattern.

## Key Information

- **Inventor**: Vannevar Bush
- **Year**: 1945 (theoretical, never built as described)
- **Form factor**: "A piece of furniture" — desk with integrated storage and display
- **Storage**: Microfilm reels with enough capacity for individual's lifetime (5000 pages/day for hundreds of years)
- **Technology basis**: 
  - Ultra high-resolution microfilm
  - Multiple screen viewers and cameras
  - Electromechanical controls
  - Dry photography for annotations
  - Coded dots on microfilm for associative links
  - Optical readers to follow associations

## Core Innovation: Associative Indexing

**Key principle**: Link any item to any other item, creating "trails" of associative connections

**Interaction model**:
1. Type on keyboard to search/retrieve
2. Use levers to flip through microfilmed documents (variable speed forward/backward)
3. Open multiple microfilms simultaneously
4. Draw lines and commentaries between them
5. Create associations between items
6. Chain associations into named "trails"
7. Copy and share trails with other users

**Trail mechanics**:
- Pulling up first item automatically pulls up associated items
- Any item can be part of multiple trails
- Trails can be named, saved, retrieved
- Trails can be copied to other people's memexes
- Trails can merge into larger trails

## Use Cases Envisioned

**Individual researcher**: Build trails connecting encyclopedias, textbooks, handbooks while researching a topic

**Professional knowledge workers**: Lawyers, patent attorneys accumulate trails over career

**Trail blazers** (new profession): Experts who find and curate valuable trails for others

**Collective knowledge**: Microfilmed encyclopedias sold with pre-built trails

## Relationship to Modern Systems

**Predicted**:
- Hypertext and hyperlinks
- Personal computers
- Internet and World Wide Web
- Wiki links and backlinks
- Graph-based knowledge management

**Realized in**:
- Obsidian graph view (trails = backlinks)
- `[[wiki links]]` (associative connections)
- LLM Wiki pattern (automated trail building)

**Key difference from modern systems**: Memex required manual trail creation; LLM Wiki uses AI to automate trail discovery and maintenance
