# As We May Think — Vannevar Bush (1945)

## Metadata

- **Type**: essay / historical document (Wikipedia article about the essay)
- **Author**: Vannevar Bush
- **Date**: July 1945 (*The Atlantic Monthly*)
- **URL**: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/As_We_May_Think
- **Historical context**: Published before and after atomic bombings of Hiroshima and Nagasaki

## Summary

Visionary 1945 essay describing the Memex, a theoretical machine for personal knowledge management based on associative indexing rather than hierarchical classification. Bush argued scientists should focus on making knowledge accessible rather than destruction, envisioning a "collective memory machine" that would transform information explosion into knowledge explosion. Anticipated hypertext, personal computers, the Internet, and modern knowledge management systems.

## Key Takeaways

**Core problem**: Information overload and poor knowledge management
> "There is a growing mountain of research. But there is increased evidence that we are being bogged down today as specialization extends. The investigator is staggered by the findings and conclusions of thousands of other workers."

**The Memex concept**:
- **Physical form**: Desk-sized machine containing microfilm storage for individual's lifetime of books, records, communications
- **Key innovation**: **Associative indexing** — link any item to any other, creating "trails" of connected knowledge
- **Interaction**: Keyboard search, levers to flip through microfilms, ability to draw connections and comments
- **Trail concept**: Chains of associations that can be named, saved, retrieved, and shared with others
- **Bi-directional**: Pulling up first item automatically pulls up associated items

**Associative vs. hierarchical indexing**:
- **Traditional (tree-like)**: Alphabetical or numerical filing, subclass to subclass, item can be in only one place
- **Associative (human-like)**: Retrieves by association, traces "trails" of information, items can be part of many trails
- **Bush's insight**: "The human mind does not operate that way, but operates by association"

**Predicted technologies**:
- Hypertext and hyperlinks
- Personal computers
- The Internet and World Wide Web
- Speech recognition
- Online encyclopedias (like Wikipedia)
- Scanner-like devices (transparent plate photography)
- Head-mounted cameras
- Brain-computer interfaces (speculative)

**Trail Blazers profession**: Bush envisioned a new job of "trail blazers" who find new and useful trails through knowledge

**Use case example**: Researcher studying Turkish bow vs. English longbow in Crusades builds trail through encyclopedias, textbooks on elasticity, then copies entire trail to friend's memex to join into broader trail about resistance to innovation

**Philosophy**: 
- Delegate routine work to machines so humans can focus on creative, intuitive work
- Transform information explosion into knowledge explosion
- Knowledge should "evolve and endure throughout the life of a race rather than of an individual"
- Need "personal information control" to function in information-saturated society

## Notable Quotes

> "The applications of science have built man a well-supplied house, and are teaching him to live healthily therein. They have enabled him to throw masses of people against one another with cruel weapons. They may yet allow him truly to encompass the great record and to grow in the wisdom of race experience."

> "Wholly new forms of encyclopedias will appear, ready-made with a mesh of associative trails running through them, ready to be dropped into the memex and there amplified."

> "When data of any sort are placed in storage, they are filed alphabetically or numerically, and information is found (when it is) by tracing it down from subclass to subclass. It can be in only one place, unless duplicates are used." — describing the limitation Bush wanted to overcome

## Integration Notes

**Historical foundation for LLM Wiki**: 
- Karpathy's pattern explicitly references Bush's Memex (1945) as conceptual ancestor
- Both emphasize associative connections over hierarchical filing
- Both envision "collective memory" systems
- Bush's "trails" ≈ modern wiki's cross-references and backlinks
- "Trail blazers" ≈ LLM maintaining and discovering connections

**Key difference**: 
- Memex: manual trail creation by user
- LLM Wiki: automated trail discovery and maintenance by LLM

**Predictions realized**:
- Hypertext → `[[wiki links]]` in Obsidian
- Associative trails → graph view, backlinks
- Microfilm storage → digital markdown files
- Collective encyclopedias with trails → Wikipedia, personal wikis

**Prediction NOT yet realized**:
- Modern file systems still largely hierarchical (FAT, NTFS, ext3) — "do not easily enable associative indexing as imagined by Bush"
- Most tools optimize for browsing, not associative retrieval
- LLM Wiki pattern is attempting to realize Bush's vision with AI assistance

**Confirms**: The ~80-year-old vision of associative knowledge management is only now becoming practical with LLMs to automate the "trail blazing" work.
